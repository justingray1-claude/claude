# DoubleTree by Hilton Signature Chocolate Chip Cookie

The official recipe — released by Hilton in 2020. This is the real thing.

## Yield
26 cookies

## Ingredients

- 1/2 lb (2 sticks / 227g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
- 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
- 3/4 cup (165g) packed light brown sugar
- 2 large eggs
- 1 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
- 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup (50g) rolled oats, ground fine in a blender or food processor
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- Pinch of cinnamon
- 2 2/3 cups (450g) semisweet chocolate chips
- 1 3/4 cups (175g) walnuts, chopped

## Instructions

### 1. Cream butter and sugars
In a stand mixer with the paddle attachment, beat softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar on medium speed for about 2 minutes until light and fluffy.

### 2. Add wet ingredients
Add eggs, vanilla, and lemon juice. Mix on low speed until fully combined. Scrape down the sides.

### 3. Mix dry ingredients
In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, ground oats, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.

### 4. Combine
Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture and mix on low until just combined. Do not overmix.

### 5. Fold in mix-ins
Stir in chocolate chips and walnuts by hand or on the lowest mixer speed.

### 6. Portion and rest
Portion dough into roughly 3 tbsp balls (~57g each). Place on a plate or tray, cover, and **refrigerate for 1 hour minimum** (overnight is even better — this is key to the flavor).

### 7. Bake
Preheat oven to **300°F (150°C)**. Yes, 300 — lower and slower than most cookie recipes.

Place dough balls on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spaced 2 inches apart. Bake for **20–23 minutes** until the edges are golden but the center is still slightly soft.

### 8. Cool
Let cookies cool on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring. They'll firm up as they cool.

## Notes

- **Ground oats** are the secret texture ingredient — they add structure and a subtle chewiness without tasting like oatmeal cookies.
- **Lemon juice** sounds weird but don't skip it. It brightens the flavor without being detectable.
- **300°F is intentional.** The low temp lets the cookies spread slowly and cook evenly all the way through.
- **Chill the dough.** Even 1 hour makes a difference. Overnight is noticeably better.
- **Walnuts are part of the original** — the hotel serves them with walnuts.
