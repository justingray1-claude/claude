# Levain Bakery-Style Chocolate Chip Walnut Cookies

These are as close as it gets to the real thing — thick, dense, gooey on the inside, slightly crisp on the outside, and enormous.

## Yield
8–10 large cookies (~6 oz each)

## Ingredients

- 1 cup (2 sticks / 227g) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
- 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
- 3/4 cup (165g) packed light brown sugar
- 2 large eggs, cold
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 3 1/2 cups (420g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 cups (340g) semisweet or dark chocolate chips
- 2 cups (200g) walnut halves, roughly chopped

## Instructions

### 1. Prep
Preheat oven to **375°F (190°C)**. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

### 2. Cream the butter and sugars
In a stand mixer with the paddle attachment, beat the **cold butter** and both sugars on medium-high speed for about 4 minutes until light and fluffy. Using cold butter is key — it keeps the cookies tall.

### 3. Add eggs and vanilla
Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. Add vanilla. Scrape down the sides.

### 4. Add dry ingredients
In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Add to the butter mixture and mix on low until *just* combined — don't overmix.

### 5. Fold in mix-ins
Add chocolate chips and walnuts, mix briefly until evenly distributed.

### 6. Portion the dough
Divide dough into 8–10 portions, roughly **6 oz each** (a kitchen scale helps). Shape them into rough, tall mounds — don't flatten. They should look chunky and tall, not like a typical cookie ball.

### 7. Bake
Bake for **11–13 minutes** until the tops and edges are golden brown but the center still looks slightly underdone. That's intentional — they firm up as they cool.

### 8. Cool
Let cookies cool on the pan for at least **10–15 minutes** before eating. They need that time to set up properly.

## Tips

- **Cold butter is non-negotiable.** Room temp butter = flat cookies.
- **Don't overbake.** Pull them when they look underdone in the center.
- **Size matters.** Going smaller will change the texture significantly — the magic is in the thick, dense structure.
- **Chill the dough** for 30–60 min before baking if you want even taller cookies.
- **Walnuts are part of the signature** — skipping them is a different cookie.

## Variations

| Variation | Swap |
|-----------|------|
| Dark Chocolate Peanut Butter | PB chips + dark chocolate chips, no walnuts |
| Oatmeal Raisin | Swap 1 cup flour for rolled oats, use raisins |
| Two-Chip | Mix dark + white chocolate chips |
